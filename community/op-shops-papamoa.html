Op Shops & Second-hand

Pāpāmoa’s charity shops, thrift stores and second-hand options - from the Waipuna Hospice shop on Domain Road to the Greerton op shop strip, 20 minutes away.

Pāpāmoa has a small but solid op shop scene, with the Waipuna Hospice shop on Domain Road the standout. For a bigger thrift shopping day, Greerton Village in Tauranga is known as New Zealand’s op shop capital with 10+ charity shops within walking distance.
Op shops in Pāpāmoa
Waipuna Hospice Charity Shop
HospiceClothingFurnitureBooks
157 Domain Road, Pāpāmoa Beach · 07 282 7143
Pāpāmoa’s best-stocked op shop. Strong daily donations mean new stock constantly arrives. The largest section is clothing, but homewares, furniture, ceramics, books, CDs and DVDs are well represented. Books, CDs and DVDs are typically $1–$3. Proceeds fund Waipuna Hospice’s specialist palliative care services. Well-organised layout and helpful staff. All purchases support local end-of-life care.
Mon–Fri: 9am–4pm · Sat–Sun: 10am–3pm

St Vincent de Paul runs several shops across Tauranga with competitive pricing and daily stock updates. Their warehouse is reportedly well-stocked - good for finding branded or quality items. Proceeds support community welfare programmes.
Worth the drive - Greerton op shops

Greerton Village, about 20 minutes from Pāpāmoa, is home to 10+ op shops clustered along Cameron Road - making it one of NZ’s best destinations for a dedicated op shop run. It’s worth making a day of it. Key shops include multiple Waipuna Hospice stores, Vinnies, Salvation Army, IHC, Habitat for Humanity ReStore, and several independent shops.

Salvation ArmyCameron Road, Greerton. Large store with clothing, furniture and homewares. salvationarmy.org.nz
Habitat for Humanity ReStoreBuilding materials, furniture, appliances and homewares at reduced prices. Proceeds fund housing for families in need.
Recycle BoutiqueBayfair Shopping Centre, Mt Maunganui. Buy and sell pre-loved branded fashion. recycleboutique.co.nz
Online second-hand - Pāpāmoa

Online platforms are increasingly popular for buying and selling second-hand in Pāpāmoa. Facebook Marketplace has active local buy/sell groups for the suburb, and Trade Me remains NZ’s largest second-hand marketplace for higher-value or niche items.

Facebook MarketplaceSearch “Pāpāmoa buy sell” for local groups. Good for furniture, appliances and clothing.
Trade MeNZ’s main marketplace. Good for anything with value. trademe.co.nz
NeighbourlyHyper-local community platform. People often give away items free to neighbours. neighbourly.co.nz
