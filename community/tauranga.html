🏙️ About Tauranga

Tauranga is a harbour city on the Bay of Plenty coast in the North Island of New Zealand, approximately 204 kilometres south-east of Auckland. It is the urban centre for a region that includes Mount Maunganui, Pāpāmoa, Te Puke and the Western Bay of Plenty. The city has grown rapidly over the past two decades, driven by lifestyle appeal, a warm climate and expanding port and logistics industries.

The Tauranga Harbour — Tauranga Moana — is one of the largest and most sheltered natural harbours in New Zealand and was of immense strategic importance to the iwi of the region, particularly Ngāi Te Rangi. The 1864 Battle of Gate Pā, fought near the city centre, was one of the most significant engagements of the New Zealand Wars. The city's Māori heritage is visible in its place names, marae and cultural institutions.

Today Tauranga is known for its beaches, café culture, outdoor recreation and growing arts scene. The CBD has undergone substantial regeneration investment and the city continues to attract new residents from across New Zealand and internationally.

🎯 Things to Do in & Around Tauranga

🏔️ Climb Mauao

The iconic Mount Maunganui — Mauao — offers a 3.4km base track with harbour and ocean views, or a harder summit climb to 232m. No dogs. 15 minutes from Pāpāmoa.

🌊 Pāpāmoa Beach

16 kilometres of white sand beach — the longest and least crowded stretch of ocean beach in the Bay of Plenty. Surfing, swimming, fishing and walks. 11km from central Tauranga.

🍷 Tauranga CBD

The regenerated city centre has a strong café, restaurant and bar scene. The Strand, Elizabeth Street and The Crossing are the main precincts. 20 minutes from Pāpāmoa.

🏄 Surfing

Pāpāmoa, Omanu and Mount Maunganui beaches all have surfable breaks. Surf schools and board hire are available at Mount Maunganui. Pāpāmoa Surf Life Saving Club patrols the beach in season.

🌿 McLaren Falls

Beautiful waterfall park 20 minutes south-west of Tauranga in the Wairoa River gorge. Night glow-worm walks available seasonally. Popular for families and walkers.

🛶 Waimarino Adventure

Kayaking, paddleboarding and aqua cycling on the Waimarino estuary near Bethlehem. Family-friendly and suitable for all levels.

⛰️ Pāpāmoa Hills

182-hectare cultural heritage regional park with ancient pā sites, walking tracks and panoramic views from the 224m summit. 20 minutes from the city. No dogs.

🎨 Tauranga Art Gallery

Free entry to the Tauranga Art Gallery on Wharf Street. Changing exhibitions featuring New Zealand and international contemporary art alongside a permanent collection.
