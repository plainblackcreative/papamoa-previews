🔄 Regular & recurring events

These events run regularly through the year. Always confirm current schedules directly with the organiser before attending.

🌊 Surf Life Saving Patrols

October – April · Weekends & public holidays

Pāpāmoa Surf Life Saving Club patrols the beach between the flags on weekends and public holidays through summer. Swim between the flags.

🏑 Touch Rugby

Summer season · Weekly

Social and competitive touch rugby at Gordon Spratt Reserve and other venues through the Tauranga Touch Association summer season.

🌅 Parkrun

Every Saturday · 8:00am

Free, timed 5km run/walk at various Tauranga locations. Check parkrun.co.nz for the current Pāpāmoa area event location and registration details.

📚 Library Story Time

Weekly — check library for current schedule

Pāpāmoa Library runs regular children’s story time sessions. Check the library schedule or Tauranga City Libraries website for current days and times.

🤼 Beach Yoga

Regular — varies by provider

Several yoga instructors run regular beach yoga sessions on Pāpāmoa Beach through summer. Check local Facebook groups and noticeboards for current schedules.

🎣 Fishing Competitions

Throughout summer

Pāpāmoa is a popular surf casting and kontiki fishing location. Competitive fishing events run throughout the summer at the eastern end of the beach. Check local fishing clubs for schedules.

📰 Local news & notices

Community notices, council announcements and local updates relevant to Pāpāmoa residents. Curated by the Papamoa.info team from public sources.

Apr 2026
Pāpāmoa Hills Regional Park partially reopens

The Summerhills Track (from Reid Road carpark) reopened on 3 April 2026 following extensive damage from January landslips. The Karangaumu Summit Track, Te Rae o Pāpāmoa Track and all other tracks remain closed. Signage is in place. Check BOPRC before visiting.

Full details on the Pāpāmoa Hills page →
Mar 2026
TCC consulting on new dog bylaw changes

Tauranga City Council opened public consultation in March 2026 on proposed changes to the Dog Management Bylaw. Proposed changes include on-lead requirements on the Pāpāmoa Shared Path, bans in cemeteries and skate parks, and licensing for walking more than four dogs at once.

Full details on the Dogs in Pāpāmoa page →
Jan 2026
Mauao (Mount Maunganui) remains closed after fatal landslide

Six people died on 22 January 2026 when a catastrophic landslide from Mauao struck the adjacent campground and hot pools during record rainfall. All Mauao tracks remain closed. The Mount Hot Pools are closed with no reopening date confirmed. The village strip, beach and businesses remain open.

Full details on the Mount Maunganui page →
Mar 2026
AIMS Games 2026 registrations open

Registrations for the Zespri AIMS Games 2026 opened on 20 March 2026. The event runs 5–11 September in Tauranga. Beyonder is the official accommodation partner. Book early — accommodation across Tauranga and Pāpāmoa fills fast during Games week.

Full details on the AIMS Games page →
