🏞️ About Gordon Spratt Reserve

Gordon Spratt Reserve is the largest and most complete public reserve in Pāpāmoa East, located on Domain Road. It serves as the primary sports, recreation and community space for the rapidly growing eastern end of Pāpāmoa — an area that has more than doubled in population since 2013.

The reserve is managed by Tauranga City Council and is used daily by local sports clubs, families, school groups and individuals. It hosts a wide range of programmed sports activity alongside informal community use throughout the week and on weekends.

Civil defence · Emergency management
🌊 Tsunami Evacuation Mound

Gordon Spratt Reserve is home to one of the Bay of Plenty's most significant civil defence assets: a purpose-built tsunami evacuation mound with capacity for approximately 3,800 people. The raised earthwork provides vertical evacuation refuge for residents of the low-lying Pāpāmoa East area who may not be able to reach higher ground in time during a tsunami warning. The mound is a prominent landmark in the reserve and is clearly signposted. In the event of a tsunami warning, residents are advised to move immediately to the mound or other high ground. Know your closest evacuation point before you need it.

⚽ Sports & Clubs

Gordon Spratt Reserve is the home ground and training base for several Pāpāmoa-based sports clubs using its marked fields and courts. Clubs operating from the reserve have included rugby league, association football (soccer), cricket and touch rugby organisations. Field lighting is available on some sections for evening training.

Changing room facilities are available for sports clubs. Clubs and schools using the reserve for organised events are advised to check field availability with Tauranga City Council Venue & Events team.
