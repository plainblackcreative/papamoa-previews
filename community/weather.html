Good to know
Pāpāmoa weather guide

☀ Sun protection

UV levels in Pāpāmoa are extreme during summer (Oct–Mar), regularly hitting 11–12 at midday. SPF 50+ sunscreen, a hat and shirt are essential when the UV index is above 3. Reapply every two hours outdoors. The UV index is shown on this page when current data is available.

🏄 Surf & swimming safety

Pāpāmoa Beach is patrolled by Pāpāmoa Surf Life Saving Club during summer (Labour Weekend to Easter). Always swim between the flags. Rips can form quickly after periods of swell or after rain. Check the Surf & Tides page before entering the water.

🌧 Rain & wind

Pāpāmoa averages around 1,300mm of rain annually — similar to Auckland but typically delivered in shorter, heavier bursts. Afternoon sea breezes are common in summer, cooling beach temperatures by 2–3°C. Northwest winds ahead of frontal systems can bring warm, humid conditions before rain arrives from the west.

⏱ Best time to visit

December through March brings the best beach conditions: warm temperatures (22–26°C), calm seas and long days. January and February are the driest months. March and April offer warm weather with far fewer crowds. The AIMS Games each September (5–11 Sep 2026) is the busiest event period — accommodation books out months ahead.

Frequently asked
Pāpāmoa weather questions
What is the weather like in Pāpāmoa? ⌄
Pāpāmoa has a warm temperate oceanic climate. Summers (December to February) are warm and often dry, with temperatures regularly reaching 25–28°C. Winters (June to August) are mild with temperatures rarely dropping below 8°C overnight. Pāpāmoa receives around 1,300mm of rain annually, spread fairly evenly across the year with no pronounced dry season.
What is the best time of year to visit Pāpāmoa? ⌄
December through to March is peak season, with long dry spells, warm sea temperatures (19–22°C) and the best conditions for swimming and surfing. March and April offer very warm weather with far fewer crowds than January and February. Winter (June to August) is mild by NZ standards — cold enough for a jacket in the evenings but not unpleasant for outdoor activities during the day.
Is Pāpāmoa Beach safe for swimming? ⌄
Pāpāmoa Beach is patrolled by Pāpāmoa Surf Life Saving Club during summer (Labour Weekend to Easter weekend). Always swim between the red and yellow flags. The beach can have rips, particularly after periods of swell or heavy rain. Water temperature is typically 19–22°C in summer and 13–16°C in winter. Check surf conditions and lifeguard flag status before entering the water.
What is the UV index like in Pāpāmoa? ⌄
UV levels in Pāpāmoa can be very high, particularly from October through to March. During summer, the UV index regularly reaches 10–12 (very high to extreme) at peak midday. Sun protection — SPF 50+ sunscreen, a wide-brimmed hat and a shirt — is recommended whenever the UV index exceeds 3. Reapply sunscreen every two hours when outdoors, and more frequently if swimming.
How does Pāpāmoa weather compare to Tauranga? ⌄
Pāpāmoa and Tauranga share essentially the same climate — Pāpāmoa is a coastal suburb on the eastern edge of Tauranga. The beach-facing aspect in Pāpāmoa means slightly more sea breeze on summer afternoons, which can cool temperatures by 2–3°C compared to the Tauranga city centre. Rain patterns and seasonal temperatures are virtually identical.
