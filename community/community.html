Community Safety & Support

Neighbourhood support networks, policing, crime reporting, emergency preparedness, and community volunteering in Pāpāmoa. Everything residents need to stay informed, connected, and prepared.
⚠️

In an Emergency, Call 111

For crimes in progress, medical emergencies, fires, or natural disasters. For non-emergency police matters, call 105 or report online at 105.police.govt.nz. If you feel a long or strong earthquake near the coast, do not wait for official warnings. Move inland immediately.

Emergency
105
Non-emergency

Pāpāmoa is generally a safe and well-connected community, but like any fast-growing area it has its own set of realities to be aware of. Property crime (particularly vehicle break-ins at beach carparks and opportunistic theft in newer subdivisions) is the most common issue. The beachfront location means tsunami preparedness is a genuine consideration, not a hypothetical one. And the rapid growth of Pāpāmoa East means some newer streets do not yet have the same level of community infrastructure as established areas.

This section of Papamoa.info brings together the practical information residents need: how the Neighbourhood Support network operates, where to report crime, what to do in each type of emergency, and how to get involved in community safety volunteering. Each topic has its own dedicated page with detailed, Pāpāmoa-specific information rather than generic advice.
