Pāpāmoa is made for weddings

From the light to the landscape, everything about this place conspires to make your day extraordinary.

🌅

The light is exceptional

Pāpāmoa's east-facing beach catches soft morning light and transforms at golden hour. Photographers consistently rate it among the Bay of Plenty's best backdrops, with views out to Motiti Island on clear days.

New Zealand's sunniest region

Planning a beach ceremony in Pāpāmoa?

Pāpāmoa Beach is one of New Zealand's most naturally beautiful ceremony settings. The long, uncrowded stretch means genuine privacy even in summer, and the views out over the bay are stunning at any time of day.

A few things to know before you start planning.

  • Permits: Check with Tauranga City Council for any permit requirements for ceremonies or structures on the beach or in reserves.
  • Timing: Early morning or late afternoon light is exceptional on this east-facing beach. Golden hour starts roughly 90 minutes before sunset.
  • Wind: Pāpāmoa can be breezy, especially in spring. Discuss wind with your stylist and florist early to plan accordingly.
  • Tides: Check the tide chart for your ceremony date. A high tide can reduce the beach width substantially.
  • Access: The beach is public. Most couples arrange a nearby venue for the reception so guests don't have to travel far post-ceremony.
  • Backup plan: Any good Pāpāmoa venue has a wet-weather option. Confirm this when you book.
  • Shoes optional: Seriously consider going barefoot. Photographers love it, and so do guests.

Local wisdom

Tips from Pāpāmoa wedding suppliers

01

Book 12–18 months out in summer

Popular venues and photographers fill fast for November through March. If your heart is set on a particular date or supplier, move early.

02

Consider a winter weekend

May to September in the Bay of Plenty can be stunning and clear. Venues offer better rates and availability is excellent. Some venues discount specifically for off-peak.

03

Use local suppliers where you can

Local photographers, florists and caterers know the venues, the light, and the logistics. That familiarity pays off on the day in ways that are hard to quantify.

04

Plan around the golden hour

Pāpāmoa's east coast orientation means the best light for photos is early morning and late afternoon. Time your ceremony to finish with 2–3 hours of natural light left.

05

Guest accommodation is easy here

Pāpāmoa has an enormous range of holiday houses and apartments. Booking an Airbnb or Bookabach for the bridal party is popular — and your photographer will love the natural light in beachside homes.

06

Motiti Island views on a clear day

On a good day the view from Pāpāmoa Beach stretches to Motiti Island and beyond. Worth building your ceremony timing around if the weather cooperates.
