🏅 What is AIMS Games?

The Zespri AIMS Games (Association of Intermediate and Middle Schools) is an annual multi-sport championship held in Tauranga, New Zealand. It is the largest sporting event for children aged 11 to 13 in New Zealand and Australasia — in 2025 it attracted more competitors than the Paris Olympics. Athletes compete in 27 different sports across venues throughout Tauranga and the Western Bay of Plenty. The event began in 2004 and has grown every year since.

Student athletes must be aged over 10 and under 13 as of 31 December 2025 — for the 2026 Games, eligible birth years are 2013, 2014 and 2015. Registrations open 20 March 2026 through school AIMS Games coordinators.

🚗 Getting Around

Most AIMS Games venues are within the Tauranga urban area, connected by the main arterials. Pāpāmoa is on State Highway 2, giving direct motorway access to Tauranga, Mount Maunganui and most venue areas. Allow extra time during Games week as traffic around Tauranga increases substantially.

Family tips for AIMS Games week

  • Download the AIMS Games app for real-time results and schedules
  • Bring camp chairs, sun hats and sunscreen to outdoor venues
  • Many venues have limited food options — pack food for competition days
  • Pāpāmoa Beach is 5 minutes away for a post-competition swim
  • Gordon Spratt Reserve is available for warm-up runs and kicks
  • Check venue start times — travel time from Pāpāmoa is 10–20 min to most venues
  • Accommodation books out — lock in Beyonder or Pacific Park now
