> **One thing I want to make the case for: keep the free Bronze listings.** I know you're not sold on giving listings away, so let me try to flip how it looks, because the free tier is genuinely the single best lead-generation tool the platform has, and it costs us almost nothing to run.
>
> The shift in perspective is this: the free listings aren't you giving value away, they're the engine that makes the whole thing make money. Three reasons:
>
> 1. **They make the directory worth visiting.** A directory with only paying businesses looks thin and empty, and an empty directory gets no traffic. Free Bronze listings fill it out so it actually covers the region, and that comprehensive coverage is exactly what makes residents use it and keep coming back. The traffic those free listings create is the product your paid advertisers are buying. No audience, nothing to sell.
> 2. **They're your upsell pipeline.** Every free business is a warm prospect already on the platform. The moment a café or a plumber sees they're getting clicks and enquiries from a free listing, upgrading to a paid tier for more exposure is an easy yes. You're not cold-selling, you're showing them proof it works. You can't upsell a business that was never listed in the first place. Free is the front door; the paid tiers are the rest of the house.
> 3. **They cost us nothing and build the SEO moat.** As I covered below, hosting a listing is ~$0. But every listing is another page Google indexes and another set of local search terms the site ranks for, like "[trade] in Papamoa" and so on. More listings means more organic traffic to the whole directory, paid listings included. A 200-business directory outranks a 20-business one every time.
>
> So the free tier isn't the thing we tolerate to be generous, it's what fills the directory, pulls the traffic, ranks in search, and feeds a steady stream of businesses we then upsell. My strong recommendation is to lead with it: get every business in the region listed for free, build the audience, then monetise the upgrades and premium placements on top.
>
> **Rolling out your other sites:** yup, very easy. The whole platform is templated: to stand up a new region we clone it, swap in the local content and branding (colours, logo, photos, categories), point it at the new domain, and it's live. Same engine underneath every site, so each one you add gets the same back end and tooling, and it gets quicker each time.
>
> **Hosting, cost and security:** this is one of the platform's real strengths. Per site, running costs are:
>
> - **Hosting:** $0/mo, the site is static and served off an enterprise edge network
> - **The dynamic bits** (forms, listings, search): $0/mo on the free tier, it covers ~100k hits a day
> - **Email** (welcome + follow-ups): $0/mo on the free tier, covers a few thousand emails a month before anything's charged
> - **Domains:** the .info you already own, around $25/yr ea
> - **Payments:** Stripe takes its standard % per sale, no monthly fee
>
> So realistically **$0–5/mo to run per site**, only creeping toward ~$25/mo if one gets seriously busy. On security and reliability: it runs on GitHub and Cloudflare's infrastructure, DDoS handled automatically, 99.9%+ uptime, and no customer or payment data sits on the site itself (Stripe holds payment details, leads sit in a locked-down sheet, and all keys live in Cloudflare's encrypted store, never in the code). Back-end support is me, I maintain the worker, the integrations and the deploys.
