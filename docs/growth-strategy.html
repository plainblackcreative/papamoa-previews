The big picture

The Site Is the Funnel

The outbound sales funnel — finding leads, sending email sequences, booking calls — is valuable and stays in place. But it should not be the only pipeline. The site itself is a parallel acquisition engine that works without any outreach effort once the content is live.

Every article, info page, and placeholder listing that ranks in Google is a silent sales pitch to the local business that should own that spot. The organic visitor who reads "Lawn Mowing Pāpāmoa" and thinks "that's my category" is a warm lead who found you — you didn't have to find them. These two tracks run in parallel and reinforce each other.

Track A
Outbound Funnel

Find businesses via Outscraper. Add to Brevo sequences. Run Day 3 / Day 6 / Day 10 follow-ups. Book a call. Show the PPP demo. Close Gold or Silver.

This track stays exactly as designed. No changes to the flow, offer structure, or sequence timing.

Track B
Organic Inbound

Google sends visitors to articles, info pages, and category listings. Some visitors are business owners in the exact category they're reading. The page catches their attention and gives them a clear path to claim their spot.

No outreach required. The content does the work.

ℹ
These tracks are complementary, not competing. A business contacted via Track A will often have already seen the site organically. A business who discovers the site via Track B may still enter the outbound sequence if they don't convert immediately. The funnel catches everyone.
Track A — unchanged

Outbound Funnel + Organic Leak Points

The funnel sequence is untouched. The only addition is "leak points" — passive CTAs at each stage that catch organic visitors who are already on the site and primed to act, without interrupting the outbound flow.

Step 1
Lead sourcing
Outscraper pulls local business data by category. Lead enters Brevo list. No site interaction required.
Step 2
Day 0 — intro email
Personalised email with article link and ad spot visible in body. Traffic number included ("this article had X reads in the last 30 days").
↳
Organic leak point: article ad spot

Any visitor who lands on that same article organically sees the same ad placement. If it's their category, a "Claim this Spotlight" link is right there. They don't need to be in the email sequence — the page catches them directly.

Step 3
Day 3 — follow-up
Reminder email. Links to the listing placeholder and the category page.
↳
Organic leak point: placeholder listing

The placeholder listing for their category ranks independently. A business searching for themselves or their category finds a placeholder with their suburb in the title, a "Spotlight available" badge, and a clear path to upgrade. No email needed.

Step 4
Day 6 — value email
Deeper value pitch. Show what a Gold listing looks like. Link to PPP if built.
Step 5
Day 10 — last-ditch
Final email. May trigger last-ditch offer (Silver price, Gold product) for selected hesitating leads.
↳
Organic leak point: category page "Spotlight available" card

Every category page shows an empty Spotlight slot when no Gold listing exists. Any business browsing their own category sees the vacancy. The card shows the spot, the price, and a link to the sales landing page. No sequence required.

Close
Call / PPP demo / payment
Gold ($1,199+GST/yr) or Silver ($599+GST/yr). TaurangaNZ.info Silver included as standard funnel bonus. Stripe payment. Onboarding sequence begins.
✓
Leak points are passive. They require no automation, no separate sequence, and no changes to the outbound flow. They are just well-placed CTAs on pages that already exist and already rank.
Track B — inbound

Organic Entry Paths

A business owner can arrive on the site via multiple organic paths and convert without ever entering the outbound sequence. Each path needs a clear, low-friction next step.

Article page
Business owner reads a relevant article
e.g. "Best Lawn Mowing Services in Pāpāmoa" — they see an ad spot mid-article and a sidebar Spotlight. The spot is clearly available. They click through to the listing landing page.
Claim spot
Category page
Business owner finds their category
They look themselves up or browse competitors. They see a "Gold Spotlight — available" card at the top of the category. One click to the sales landing page.
Claim spot
Placeholder listing
Business owner finds their own placeholder
A generic listing for their subcategory ranks in Google. They recognise it as their category. The listing has a "This could be your business" CTA with traffic data shown ("X enquiries via this listing in the last 30 days").
Upgrade listing
Web3Forms lead
Enquiry submitted via a placeholder listing

Follow up
Direct search
Business owner Googles their own business name
The placeholder or category page ranks for "[Business Name] Pāpāmoa." They find a listing that already looks like theirs. The "Claim this listing" CTA is prominent.
Claim listing
⚠
All organic CTAs point to /sales/landing.html. Every "Claim this Spotlight", "Upgrade listing", and "List your business" link across articles, category pages, and placeholder listings must use a consistent destination. Check that all current placeholder files use this path, not href="#".
The foundation

Placeholder Engine

Placeholders are not filler. They are the primary organic acquisition asset. Every empty subcategory gets a placeholder that does all of the following simultaneously:

1

Ranks in Google

Written in directory voice with full SEO structure, local keywords, schema markup. Every subcategory has a page — no empty slots.

2

Captures visitor enquiries

Web3Forms contact form routes to jkbrownnz@gmail.com. Every enquiry is evidence of traffic value that can be shown to a prospect.

3

Generates traffic data

Google Analytics tracks page views per listing. "690 reads in 23 days" is a concrete sales number. No placeholder = no data.

4

Shows the vacant Spotlight

The "Gold Spotlight — available" badge is visible on every placeholder. Any business who finds it sees the opportunity immediately.

5

Outreach ammunition

Traffic and enquiry data from placeholders is the primary sales tool for the outbound sequence. The placeholder runs before outreach begins.

6

Inbound self-qualification

A business owner who finds their own placeholder is already pre-sold on the concept. They found you. The conversion barrier is the lowest it will ever be.

ℹ
Build order matters. Placeholders need to be live before outreach starts — ideally 2-4 weeks before — so there is real traffic data to reference in the intro email. "This placeholder has had X views" is the most compelling line in the sequence. An empty slot produces nothing.

Summary

One-Paragraph Version

Build the site first: articles, info pages, and a placeholder listing in every subcategory. Get them live and indexed. Start collecting traffic numbers. Then run the outbound sequence using those numbers as proof. Meanwhile, every page that ranks is also a passive sales pitch to any business owner who finds it organically — via article ad spots, category Spotlight vacancies, and placeholder "claim this listing" CTAs. The outbound funnel stays exactly as designed. The organic layer doesn't replace it, it runs underneath it and feeds the same destination. Both tracks close to the same sales page, the same PPP demo, the same Gold or Silver product.
