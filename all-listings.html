Barber Toms

Neighbourhood barbershop in Pāpāmoa and Mount Maunganui
80 Parton Road, Pāpāmoa Also at 1 Newton Street, Mt Maunganui

About This Business

Barber Toms is a walk-in-friendly barbershop on Parton Road that has built a solid following around straightforward service and a relaxed atmosphere. They handle men's and kids' cuts, beard trims, razor fades, and a full "The Works" package that covers everything in one sitting. Bookings are taken through Fresha, and they also accept walk-ins when chairs are free.

The shop also carries its own range of grooming products available through their online store. A second location recently opened on Newton Street in Mount Maunganui, giving Mount locals the same service without the drive out to Pāpāmoa. Both shops maintain the same pricing and booking system.

Worth knowing: Barber Toms is a barber rather than a unisex salon, so the service is focused on men's and boys' cuts. The vibe is casual and unpretentious, which regulars seem to appreciate.

★ Gold Spotlight

ReDefined Wellbeing Hub

Award-winning gym, pilates studio, and recovery centre in Pāpāmoa
Pāpāmoa Also in Takapuna, Auckland Exercise NZ Award Winner

About This Business

ReDefined is more than a gym, though calling it a "wellbeing hub" only partly captures what they have assembled under one roof. The Pāpāmoa studio houses five specialised spaces: a reformer pilates studio, a yoga and meditation room, a functional strength and conditioning area, an open gym, and a dedicated recovery lab. On-site health practitioners cover nutrition, physiotherapy, massage, and counselling.

They won the Exercise New Zealand Supreme Facility of the Year award in 2023, which gives some indication of how the fitness industry views what they have built. Membership is structured as a single pass that covers all studios and classes rather than charging separately for each discipline. That model works well for people who want variety without juggling multiple subscriptions.

The atmosphere leans towards community-driven rather than hardcore gym culture. ReDefined clearly pitches itself at people looking for long-term habits rather than short-term intensity. Their trial offer lets newcomers test the full range of classes before committing. It is worth noting the Pāpāmoa location can get busy during peak morning and evening class slots, so booking ahead is recommended.

★ Silver Listing

Coastal Autos

One-stop automotive workshop in Pāpāmoa
Pāpāmoa 07 282 7100 MTA Approved

About This Business

Coastal Autos positions itself as a one-stop shop, and the service list backs that up. They handle WOFs, full vehicle servicing, tyres, wheel alignments, brakes, auto electrical work, air conditioning, batteries, transmission, suspension, diagnostics, and even vehicle licensing. That last one is convenient: you can get your WOF and handle the rego paperwork in the same visit rather than making a separate trip.

The workshop is MTA approved, which provides a baseline of industry standards and access to dispute resolution if anything goes wrong. They also handle fleet servicing, which suggests the operation is set up to manage volume and scheduling rather than working purely on a one-car-at-a-time basis.

The team makes a point of presenting themselves as people-first rather than purely transactional. Reviews tend to mention the communication and transparency around what work is actually needed, which matters in an industry where trust can be an issue. Online booking is available through their website.

Blackberry Eatery

All-day cafe and evening dining at Fashion Island, Pāpāmoa
Shop 1/42 Gravatt Road, Fashion Island 07 572 1724

About This Business

Blackberry Eatery sits inside Fashion Island on Gravatt Road and runs a split-format operation: cafe-style breakfast and lunch during the day, then a more substantial dinner service Thursday through Sunday evenings. The coffee programme is built around Allpress beans with trained baristas, and the cabinet food is made in-house.

The dinner menu covers a broader range than you might expect from a daytime cafe. Previous menus have featured items like lamb shanks, pasta dishes, and dumplings alongside more standard evening fare. They also mix handcrafted cocktails and carry a wine list, so the evening offering is a genuine sit-down dining experience rather than a cafe that stays open late.

Functions are a meaningful part of the business. They can seat up to 120 people for private events and offer buffet, cocktail, and working lunch formats. The venue is fully licensed with both indoor and outdoor seating. Takeaway and delivery are available with online ordering, and they occasionally run promotions through their website.

The Wagon

Gourmet burgers, bar, and grill in Pāpāmoa
Pāpāmoa 07 262 0123 Walk-ins welcome

About This Business

The Wagon is a burger-focused bar and grill with locations in Pāpāmoa and Whakatane. The Pāpāmoa branch leans into a lively, social atmosphere and is one of those spots that fills up on weekends without much effort. Walk-ins are welcome, but for groups of six or more they recommend booking ahead by phone or email.

The menu centres on gourmet burgers, though they run a broader grill menu alongside it. They accept online orders for takeaway through their website. If the restaurant is at capacity when you arrive, they offer a callback system so you do not have to wait around.

As a small chain with two locations, The Wagon benefits from some operational consistency. The Pāpāmoa spot has built a strong local following. It is a solid option for casual dining where the focus is on the food and the atmosphere rather than fine dining formality.

The Pizza Pundits

Creative pizzas with a twist, Pāpāmoa Beach Road
1/898 Pāpāmoa Beach Road 07 542 0058 Open Wed - Sun from 4pm

About This Business

The Pizza Pundits sits on Pāpāmoa Beach Road and takes a more creative approach to pizza than the standard takeaway. Their point of difference is the menu, which blends traditional pizza bases with toppings that draw from Indian and fusion cooking. Items like butter paneer pizza and apricot-based flavour combinations set them apart from the franchise pizza shops in the area.

They cater to dietary needs more thoughtfully than most pizza places. Vegan and gluten-free bases are available, and the menu is designed so those options still carry genuine flavour rather than feeling like afterthoughts. Standard classics are on the menu too for anyone who just wants a reliable margherita or meat lovers.

The operation is fairly small and intimate. They are closed Monday and Tuesday, opening from 4pm Wednesday through Sunday. This is primarily an evening spot, making it a good option for a post-beach dinner or takeaway run. Delivery is available through their online ordering system.

Pro Supplements

Sports nutrition and supplement retailer, Tauranga
45 Grey Street, Tauranga In-store only (no Pāpāmoa location)

About This Business

Pro Supplements operates from Grey Street in Tauranga's CBD and carries a broad range of sports nutrition products. Their shelves cover the main categories: whey protein (isolate, casein, plant-based), pre-workouts, creatine, BCAAs and amino acids, test boosters, vitamins, weight loss supplements, and accessories. Brands stocked include Optimum Nutrition, EHP Labs, Pack Nutrition, Muscletech, and others.

The store runs an online shop alongside the physical location, though the site emphasises that featured products are available in-store at Grey Street. They regularly run clearance deals and carry new arrivals, so stock rotates more frequently than at some larger chains. Pricing appears competitive with online-only supplement retailers, particularly on their sale items.

It is worth noting this is a Tauranga CBD business rather than a Pāpāmoa-based one. For Pāpāmoa residents, it is a short drive into town. The store is independent rather than franchise-owned, which typically means more flexibility on product advice and recommendations compared to chain supplement shops.

Hakanini Surf School & Rentals - Pāpāmoa Domain
★ Gold Spotlight

Hakanini Surf School & Rentals

Pāpāmoa's surf school at the Domain, lessons and board hire for all ages
Pāpāmoa Domain, 559 Pāpāmoa Beach Road Lessons from $70

About This Business

Hakanini is Pāpāmoa's dedicated surf school, operating from the Domain at the heart of the beach. Founded by Tom Botting, who brings international experience from running surf rentals and surfing the big waves of Pichilemu in Chile, the school has become a local fixture and built a reputation that extends well beyond Pāpāmoa.

They run group lessons for up to eight people, private one-on-one sessions, and school holiday programmes that typically run for a week at an hour per day. Group lessons cover two hours and start from around $70 depending on group size. Board hire is available separately at $35 for a half day or $45 for a full day, which makes it one of the more affordable rental options in the Bay of Plenty.

The teaching approach focuses on ocean safety and wave understanding alongside the surfing itself, which parents tend to appreciate. Reviews consistently mention the instructors' ability to build confidence in kids and beginners, with multiple reviewers noting visible improvement even within a single session. The school's location near the Pāpāmoa Beach Holiday Resort makes it particularly convenient for holidaymakers.

Hakanini operates on a seasonal schedule that ramps up significantly over summer. During peak periods, group lessons run at 10am and 1pm daily. Availability can be limited during school holidays, so booking early is recommended for holiday programmes.

PAP House

Restaurant, bar, and local gathering spot on Pāpāmoa Beach Road
1072 Pāpāmoa Beach Road 07 542 0365 Open 7 days Dog friendly

About This Business

PAP House describes itself as "the local's living room," and the description fits. This is a restaurant and bar on Pāpāmoa Beach Road that has become one of those places where regulars show up multiple times a week without it feeling like a special occasion. The atmosphere is social and relaxed, with both indoor and outdoor seating areas.

The menu caters broadly, with gluten-free, dairy-free, vegetarian, and vegan options clearly marked. They serve from morning through to late evening, seven days a week, with Saturday and Sunday opening an hour earlier at 9am. The kitchen handles everything from casual drinks and bar snacks through to full meals, making it versatile enough for a coffee catch-up, a family dinner, or a Friday night out.

Dogs are welcome, which adds to the casual, neighbourhood feel. Table bookings can be made online, and they also run pickup and delivery through their ordering platform. Gift cards are available for purchase through the website.

The venue has built strong word-of-mouth locally. Reviews tend to focus on the consistency of the food and the welcoming atmosphere rather than any single standout dish, which is often the mark of a place that gets the fundamentals right across the board.
