Shopping in Pāpāmoa — common questions

Pāpāmoa has three main retail centres. Pāpāmoa Plaza on Gravatt Road is anchored by Woolworths and has specialty stores, pharmacy, cafes and services. Fashion Island at 42 Gravatt Road is a factory outlet village with 18+ stores including Cotton On, Puma, Under Armour, Skechers and Decjuba. The Sands at Pāpāmoa East opened in stages from late 2024 and includes a New World supermarket, with a Mitre 10 MEGA under construction for 2026. Bayfair Mall in Mount Maunganui is about 15 minutes away and is the region’s largest indoor mall.
Xanadu Book Exchange at 2C Ashley Place, Pāpāmoa East, is the Bay of Plenty’s only warehouse-scale second-hand bookstore. It stocks around 10,000 books at any time, colour-coded by price with no book over $5. It also stocks DVDs, CDs, vinyl records and jigsaws. Owner Briar Simons pays cash or store credit for quality used books.
Yes. Mitre 10 Pāpāmoa is currently at 14 Gravatt Road. A new Mitre 10 MEGA store of approximately 10,580 square metres is under construction at The Sands town centre in Pāpāmoa East, expected to open in 2026. The current store will close once the MEGA opens.
Backdoor Surf at Pāpāmoa Plaza and Switch Stance on Ashley Place both stock boards, wetsuits, surf accessories and skate gear. Hakanini Surf School on the Pāpāmoa Domain also hires boards and equipment by the hour or day.
Pāpāmoa has three supermarkets. Woolworths is at Pāpāmoa Plaza on Gravatt Road. PAK’nSAVE is nearby on Gravatt Road. New World The Sands opened at Pāpāmoa East in late 2024. There are also convenience stores serving the eastern end of the suburb.
Pāpāmoa Motorsports on Ashley Place stocks motorcycles, jetskis and quad bikes. They carry new and used stock and offer servicing. Ashley Place is an industrial precinct off Parton Road in eastern Pāpāmoa.
