Shopping in Pāpāmoa - common questions

Pāpāmoa has three main retail centres. Pāpāmoa Plaza on Gravatt Road is anchored by a supermarket with specialty stores, a pharmacy, cafes and services. Fashion Island is a factory-outlet village with 18+ stores. The Sands at Pāpāmoa East opened in stages from late 2024 with a supermarket and a large hardware store under construction for 2026. Bayfair Mall in Mount Maunganui is about 15 minutes away.
Pāpāmoa East has the Bay of Plenty’s only warehouse-scale second-hand bookstore - around 10,000 books at any time, colour-coded by price with no book over $5, plus DVDs, CDs, vinyl records and jigsaws. See the Books & Second-hand page.
Yes. Pāpāmoa has a hardware and building-supplies store on Gravatt Road, and a large new hardware megastore of around 10,580 square metres is under construction at the Pāpāmoa East town centre, expected to open in 2026. See the Building Supplies page.
Pāpāmoa has surf and skate shops stocking boards, wetsuits, surf accessories and skate gear, and surf schools on the Pāpāmoa Domain hire boards and equipment by the hour or day. See the Surf & Skate page.
Pāpāmoa has three supermarkets - two near Pāpāmoa Plaza on Gravatt Road and one at the Pāpāmoa East town centre (opened late 2024) - plus convenience stores serving the eastern end. See the Supermarkets page.
Pāpāmoa has a powersports dealer on Ashley Place stocking motorcycles, jetskis and quad bikes, new and used, with servicing. Ashley Place is an industrial precinct off Parton Road in eastern Pāpāmoa. See the Powersports page.
