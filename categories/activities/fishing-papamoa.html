Beach Fishing in Pāpāmoa

Pāpāmoa Beach is one of the Bay of Plenty’s most popular surf-casting locations. The beach runs for 16 kilometres with multiple access points - blue “beach access” posts mark the paths through the dunes. No licence is required for recreational saltwater fishing in New Zealand. Key species include snapper, kahawai and trevally, with catches varying by season and tide. Evening incoming tides are generally the most productive for surf casting.

Can you fish at Pāpāmoa Beach?+
Yes. Pāpāmoa Beach is a popular surf-casting spot with multiple access points along its 16km length. Snapper, kahawai and trevally are the main catches. No fishing licence is required for recreational saltwater fishing in New Zealand.
What fish can you catch at Pāpāmoa?+
The main species caught from Pāpāmoa Beach are snapper, kahawai and trevally. Kahawai are present year-round, while snapper and trevally are more seasonal. Evening incoming tides are generally best for surf casting.
Are there fishing charters near Pāpāmoa?+
Charter boat fishing is available from the wider Bay of Plenty, operating out of Tauranga Harbour. Half-day and full-day trips targeting snapper and kingfish are common. See the fishing guide for current operator options.
