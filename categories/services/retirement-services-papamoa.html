Premium over-55 village at 84 Simpson Road, Pāpāmoa - one street from the beach and one of the Bay of Plenty’s most desirable retirement addresses. Karaka Pines operates on a capital gains model: residents retain their share of property appreciation, with a 12.5% exit fee on departure. Rest home subsidy - what you need to know

New Zealanders who need rest home care may be eligible for a rest home subsidy from Work and Income (WINZ). Eligibility depends on your assets and income. To check eligibility and apply, contact Work and Income directly. A means assessment is required. Age Concern and Citizens Advice Bureau can also provide guidance.

Common Questions
Are there retirement villages in Pāpāmoa?▼
Yes. Karaka Pines at 84 Simpson Road is Pāpāmoa’s premium over-55 village, one street from the beach. It operates on a capital gains model - residents retain their share of property appreciation, less a 12.5% exit fee. Homes range from 2-bedroom duplexes to 3-bedroom standalone. Contact Trudi on 027 230 2997.
What is a capital gains model retirement village?▼
In a capital gains model, residents share in any increase in the property’s value over time. When leaving, they receive the appreciation minus a deferred management fee (exit fee). At Karaka Pines the exit fee is 12.5%. This contrasts with an Occupation Right Agreement (ORA) model where residents do not benefit from capital gain.
What is the difference between a retirement village and aged care?▼
Retirement villages are independent living communities for active older adults, typically over 55. Residents live independently in their own home within the village. Aged care (rest homes and care homes) provides nursing and personal care for those who need daily assistance. Some villages offer a continuum of care, allowing residents to transition to care as needed.
Does Pāpāmoa have aged care facilities?▼
Pāpāmoa has a growing aged care sector reflecting its population. For rest home subsidy information, contact Work and Income (WINZ). Age Concern Tauranga can also provide independent guidance on aged care options in the Bay of Plenty.
