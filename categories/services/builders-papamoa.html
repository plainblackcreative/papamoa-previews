LBP licensing: what you need to know

All Restricted Building Work (RBW) in New Zealand - which includes most structural work on homes - must be carried out or supervised by a Licensed Building Practitioner (LBP). Before engaging any builder for consent work, confirm their LBP licence number and check it is current at lbp.govt.nz.

Common Questions
Do builders in Pāpāmoa need to be LBP licensed?▼
Yes. Restricted Building Work (RBW) - which covers most structural work on homes - must be carried out or supervised by a Licensed Building Practitioner (LBP). Confirm any builder’s LBP licence at lbp.govt.nz before engaging for consent work.
How much does it cost to build a house in Pāpāmoa?▼
New build construction costs in the Bay of Plenty typically range from $2,500 to $4,000+ per square metre depending on specification, site conditions and current material prices. Budget builds on flat sections start at the lower end; high-spec coastal homes sit above $3,500/m². Always get a fixed-price contract or detailed schedule of quantities before proceeding.
Can a builder in Pāpāmoa do a minor dwelling or granny flat?▼
Yes. Minor dwellings and sleepouts are common project types in Pāpāmoa given the large sections in newer subdivisions. They require building consent. Most builders listed here can quote. Check with Tauranga City Council about setbacks and site coverage rules for your specific section.
What is the build time for a new house in Pāpāmoa?▼
Typical build time for a new home in Pāpāmoa ranges from 6 to 14 months from consent issue to code compliance certificate, depending on size and complexity. Tauranga City Council processes standard consent applications in 20 working days. Factor in design and consent time on top of the construction period.
