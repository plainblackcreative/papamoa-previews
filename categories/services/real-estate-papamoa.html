Real Estate in Pāpāmoa — common questions

Yes. Multiple real estate agencies and independent agents operate in Pāpāmoa covering residential sales, auctions and private treaty. Major national brands including Ray White, Harcourts, Bayleys and others all have agents active in the suburb. Pāpāmoa is one of New Zealand’s fastest-growing residential areas and sees strong year-round transaction volumes.
Pāpāmoa property values vary significantly by location, property type and proximity to the beach. Entry-level properties in the eastern end can be more affordable, while beachside and Golden Sands homes command premium prices. For current valuations and recent sales data, consult a local Pāpāmoa real estate agent or check listings on trademe.co.nz/property.
Pāpāmoa has been one of the Bay of Plenty’s strongest residential markets for over a decade. The suburb has grown from around 12,000 residents in 2010 to 40,000+ today, driven by affordability relative to Tauranga CBD, lifestyle appeal and strong infrastructure investment. The Sands development in Pāpāmoa East continues to add new sections and housing stock.
Pāpāmoa is consistently rated as one of the Bay of Plenty’s most popular residential suburbs. Beach access, good schools, a growing local amenity base and relatively more affordable land prices than Mount Maunganui are the main drawcards. Growth areas include Golden Sands and Pāpāmoa East around The Sands development.
Contact agencies listed here directly, or browse active listings on realestate.co.nz and trademe.co.nz/property to see which agents are most active in your area of Pāpāmoa. Many agents specialise in specific streets or subdivisions, so local knowledge matters.
The Sands in Pāpāmoa East is the suburb’s largest current development, adding a new town centre (including New World and a forthcoming Mitre 10 MEGA), new sections and housing. Golden Sands continues to develop in the mid-suburb. These developments continue to drive population growth and property demand.
