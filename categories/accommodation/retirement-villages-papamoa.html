📋 Retirement Living in Pāpāmoa

Why Pāpāmoa for retirement?

  • Flat beach: Pāpāmoa’s 16km beach is one of the most accessible in the Bay of Plenty — flat, wide and easy to walk along.
  • Community: Strong community infrastructure with local shops, cafes, parks and services within walking distance in most parts of Pāpāmoa.
  • Hospital access: Tauranga Hospital is approximately 20 minutes by car. Bay of Plenty private specialists are also accessible from Pāpāmoa.
  • Growth area: Pāpāmoa East and Golden Sands continue to develop, with new retail, dining and community facilities coming online.
  • Lifestyle villages vs retirement villages: Pāpāmoa has both. Karaka Pines operates on a capital gains ownership model. Traditional occupancy-right retirement villages may also be available — always check the ownership structure before committing.

Information on this page is general only. Before entering into any retirement village agreement, seek independent legal advice. The Retirement Villages Act 2003 governs the rights and responsibilities of all parties.

❓ Common Questions
Are there retirement villages in Pāpāmoa?▼
Yes. Karaka Pines Pāpāmoa at 84 Simpson Road is a premium retirement village one street from the beach and two minutes from The Sands Town Centre. Contact Trudi on 027 230 2997 for availability and pricing. Additional retirement and lifestyle village operators can claim listings on this page.
What is the difference between a retirement village and a lifestyle village?▼
Retirement villages in New Zealand are governed by the Retirement Villages Act 2003 and typically offer occupancy rights rather than ownership. Lifestyle villages (sometimes called over-55 or land-lease communities) often allow residents to own their home on leased land. Karaka Pines operates on a capital gains ownership model — contact them directly for specifics. Always seek independent legal advice before signing any village agreement.
What is the capital gains model at Karaka Pines Pāpāmoa?▼
Karaka Pines operates on a capital gains model where residents retain their share of property value increase. An exit fee of 12.5% applies on departure. This differs from traditional occupancy-right retirement villages where residents typically do not benefit from capital gains. Contact Trudi on 027 230 2997 for full details on the ownership and exit structure.
Is Pāpāmoa good for retirees?▼
Pāpāmoa offers a flat, accessible beach, strong community infrastructure, good access to Tauranga Hospital and growing retail and dining options. It is consistently rated among the most desirable coastal suburbs in the Bay of Plenty for retirees.
How close is Karaka Pines to Pāpāmoa Beach?▼
Karaka Pines at 84 Simpson Road is one street from Pāpāmoa Beach and two minutes from The Sands Town Centre. It is one of the most beachside-positioned retirement villages in the Bay of Plenty.
