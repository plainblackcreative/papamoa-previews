About Pacific Park Christian Holiday Camp

Pacific Park Christian Holiday Camp has operated near Pāpāmoa Beach since 1944, founded as Mount Bible Class Camp by two young men responding to what they saw as the moral influence of summer holiday culture on New Zealand youth. Today it runs as a fully equipped, family-friendly holiday park just a two-minute walk from the beach.

The park is smoke, vape, drug and alcohol-free. Many reviewers — including those who specifically note they are not Christian — cite this as a positive, praising the calm and family-focused atmosphere. Accommodation ranges from powered campsites to air-conditioned two-bedroom cabins, fully serviced motel units, and self-contained group lodges.

Facilities include a large 40-degree hot tub, jumping pillow, children’s playground, games room, communal kitchens, BBQ areas and a conference centre for events, retreats and school camps. As a non-profit, Pacific Park supports community ministries and charities, and offers ministry discounts to qualifying organisations. Reception is open 8:30am–6:30pm seven days, with late arrivals accommodated on request.

Frequently asked questions
Is Pacific Park alcohol-free? ▼
Yes. Pacific Park is smoke, vape, drug and alcohol-free. This is one of the most frequently mentioned positives in reviews — including from guests who note they are not religious. The environment is calm and family-focused as a result.
Do you need to be Christian to stay at Pacific Park? ▼
No. Pacific Park welcomes all guests regardless of faith. Many reviewers specifically mention they are not Christian but chose Pacific Park for its clean, family-friendly and peaceful environment. No religious participation is required.
What accommodation types are available? ▼
Pacific Park offers powered campsites, unserviced sites, air-conditioned two-bedroom cabins, fully serviced motel-style units, and self-contained group lodges. A conference centre is available for larger events and retreats.
Where is Pacific Park located? ▼
Pacific Park is at 1110 Pāpāmoa Beach Road — a two-minute walk from Pāpāmoa Beach and close to Fashion Island and Palm Beach Plaza shopping centres.
Is Pacific Park suitable for school camps? ▼
Yes. Pacific Park has group lodge accommodation and a conference centre suited to school camps, sports teams, church groups and youth organisations. Group rates are available — contact the office to discuss requirements.
