About Mumbai Masala

Mumbai Masala operates from a compact but well-run kitchen on Papamoa Beach Road, turning out the kind of curry-house staples that have made Indian food a fixture in New Zealand dining — butter chicken, lamb rogan josh, saag paneer — alongside a wider menu of regional dishes that reward repeat visits. The tandoor oven is central to the operation: naan bread comes out blistered and fresh to order, and the tandoori chicken has a following among regulars.

The vegetarian and vegan menu is genuinely broad rather than an afterthought, with a dozen dishes that cover both the classics (chana masala, dal makhani) and a few less common options. Biryani is available in chicken, lamb, prawn and vegetable versions and can be ordered for groups with enough notice. The takeaway operation runs efficiently alongside dine-in service, with most orders out in under 25 minutes.

Mumbai Masala sits in the casual mid-range — portions are generous, the spice level is adjustable on request, and the BYO policy keeps the cost of a full meal reasonable. It is a reliable choice for a Friday night takeaway or a sit-down dinner for groups who want something with more variety than a single cuisine focus.
