About This Business

Speedy Screens is a Pāpāmoa-based business specialising in three distinct product lines: custom insect screens, security doors and windows, and built-in wardrobe solutions. All products are made to measure rather than off-the-shelf, which means they handle non-standard openings and unusual configurations that generic screen products cannot.

The insect screening side of the operation covers retractable, pleated, sliding, and fixed screen options for doors and windows. They use Amplimesh products, which are an established brand in the NZ screening market. For security, they supply and install Supascreen and similar heavy-duty mesh systems that provide both insect protection and physical security without the prison-bar look of older-style security doors.

The wardrobe division is a less obvious offering from a screen company, but it stems from the same joinery and custom fabrication capability. They build wall-hung and built-in wardrobes with sliding doors, custom shelving, and internal fittings like pull-out shoe racks and drawer towers.

Owner-operator Rob brings over 16 years of experience in the joinery and screen industry. The "speedy" in the name refers to turnaround rather than rushing the work. They offer free on-site consultations where they measure up and quote, then manufacture and install. It is worth noting that while the business is based in Pāpāmoa, their service area extends across the entire Bay of Plenty, from Waihi through to Waihau Bay. Repair and remeshing of existing screens is also available, which can be a more cost-effective option than full replacement for older homes.

Common Questions

What types of insect screens do they install?
They supply and fit retractable (pleated) screens, sliding screens, and fixed mesh screens for both doors and windows. All products are custom measured for each opening, so they work with non-standard sizes and shapes. The retractable options fold away when not in use, which suits modern bifold and stacker door setups.
How do security screens differ from standard fly screens?
Security screens use heavier-gauge stainless steel or aluminium mesh (like the Supascreen system) that resists forced entry while still allowing airflow and visibility. They look similar to standard screens from a distance but provide genuine physical security. Useful for ground-floor doors and windows, particularly in homes that are left open during summer.
What is the wardrobe service about?
Speedy Screens builds custom wardrobe interiors with sliding doors, shelving, drawers, and fittings like pull-out shoe racks. The work comes from the same joinery skill set as their screen installations. They build wall-hung and floor-to-ceiling configurations, typically in new builds or renovation projects.
Do they repair existing screens?
Yes. They offer repair and remeshing services for damaged screens, which can extend the life of existing frames without the cost of a full replacement. This is a practical option for older homes where the frame is still in good condition but the mesh has torn or degraded.
How far do they travel?
Their stated coverage runs from Waihi in the west through to Waihau Bay in the east, covering the entire Bay of Plenty region. For distant locations, it may take longer to schedule an on-site visit, but they do service rural and remote properties across this range.
